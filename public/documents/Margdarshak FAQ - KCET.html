Karnataka Common Entrance Test - Complete Guide

KCET & Karnataka Engineering Admission: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is KCET?
KCET (Karnataka Common Entrance Test) is the entrance exam conducted by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) for admission to engineering, pharmacy, architecture, and several other courses in Karnataka's state and private colleges.
2. Who is eligible for KCET? What documents do I need?
Eligibility Criteria for Karnataka CET:
  • Student should have studied in Karnataka school (any board – state/CBSE/ICSE) for minimum 7 years (complete academic years and not mid-term of a particular year out of the 7 years)
  • Of these 7 years (or more) – at least one of the year should be either in 10th or 12th
  • Must have passed 12th/2nd PUC with Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (PCM) or (PCB for some courses)
Examples to know if you are eligible or not for KCET:
  • NOT Eligible: A student has studied for 9 years from 1st standard till 9th standard and moved to a different state from 10th to 12th standard – NOT Eligible for KCET – as student has not done one of the board exams of 10th or 12th from Karnataka
  • Eligible: A student has studied from 1st standard to 6th standard (full year) in Karnataka. Then 7th to 11th standard in some other state. Again the student has studied 12th in Karnataka – Eligible for KCET and it meets both the above mentioned criteria (minimum 7 years + one of the board exams in Karnataka)
  • NOT Eligible: A student has studied from LKG to 6th standard in Karnataka and 7th onwards in a different state – NOT eligible for KCET as the academic years are considered only between 1st standard and 12th standard, out of which one need to have studied at least 7 years in Karnataka
Required Documents:

To prove that you have studied in Karnataka for at least 7 years and completed either 10th or 12th in Karnataka, KEA has started using SATS (Students Achievement Tracking System) to pull the data automatically for your ward. The SATS (or sometimes referred to as STS ID) should be part of TC which your school would have issued. Otherwise, you need to contact the school and get the ID. There can be more than one SATS ID also (when kids changed school, the new school would have created a new SATS ID) which is acceptable by KCET.

In some cases, where SATS entry is not available for your kid for some years which they have studied in Karnataka, you need to get study certificate from school (in the format which KEA has mandated). Please note that the KEA has a specific format with name, year(s) of study, board, mother tongue etc which should be mentioned in study certificate. Hence any other document like TC cannot be used as study certificate.

If a student has studied from 1st standard to 4th standard in one school in Karnataka, then from 5th to 10th is another school and 11th/12th in a different college/school – then you need to go to each of those schools and get study certificate for the duration of student's study in that school/board. If any of these schools belong to state board, then you need to go to BEO office corresponding to the school (you check with respective school) and get the study certificate attested from BEO (block education officer). KEA/KCET don't expect CBSE/ICSE study certificates to be attested though.

Non-Karnataka students can only apply through COMEDK.

3. How is the KCET rank calculated?
The final rank is based on 50% of the KCET exam marks (PCM subjects) and 50% of the qualifying board exam marks (PCM).
4. Are CBSE/ICSE/Other Board marks accepted for KCET ranking?
Yes, the PCM marks from any recognized board (CBSE, ICSE, Cambridge, State Board, etc.) are considered for calculating the 50% board weightage.
5. Is biology required for BTech Biotechnology?
Most BTech Biotechnology programs in Karnataka require only PCM for eligibility, similar to other engineering branches. For pure science degrees (like BS/MS in IISc), biology might be needed. Always check the specific college's requirements.
6. What are the required documents for KCET registration and counseling?
  • SSLC/10th marks card (for proof of age and education)
  • 12th/PUC marks card
  • Study certificate (from classes 1–12, especially 7 years in Karnataka)
  • Caste/category certificate (if claiming reservation)
  • Domicile/Residence certificate (if needed)
  • SATS DB (Student Achievement Tracking System – for students in Karnataka state schools)
  • Recent passport-size photos and photo ID proof
7. What is SATS DB or SATS Id? Why is it important?
SATS (Student Achievement Tracking System) is a database of all Karnataka state students. Students from Karnataka state schools must provide their unique SATS ID for admissions, document verification, and tracking.
8. What if my board marks and name spelling differ across documents?
Name, date of birth, and other personal details must match across all major documents (Aadhaar, board marks cards, study certificates). Discrepancies must be rectified before document verification; a legal affidavit or correction certificate may be required.
9. Do foreign/OCI students qualify for KCET?
Typically, only Indian citizens or those who have valid 7-year study certificates in Karnataka (including a board exam year) can apply for KCET. Some OCI students have been allowed if they meet domicile/study requirements.
10. What is STS or Study Certificate? How is it obtained?
The Study Certificate proves the student has studied for the required number of years (at least 7) in Karnataka. It is issued by the school principal/head, often on a standard format prescribed by KEA.
11. What if I studied some years in Karnataka, but not all?
Only students who complete 7 years (including 10th/12th) in Karnataka are eligible for state quota through KCET. Others should apply using COMEDK/all-India or management quotas.
12. Is there negative marking in KCET?
No, there is no negative marking in KCET.
13. How many times can I attempt KCET?
You can attempt KCET multiple times, but only during your class 12th year or the year you complete 12th/2nd PUC.
14. When is KCET conducted and when is the application deadline?
KCET is usually conducted in May. The application window typically opens in February/March and closes in March/April. Check the KEA website for exact dates.
15. Does Karnataka have reservations in engineering admissions?
Yes, Karnataka has various categories: GM, SC, ST, 2A/2B/3A/3B, Rural, Kannada Medium, Hyderabad-Karnataka, and more. Each has its quota, and documentary proof must be provided.
16. How and when do I register for KCET?
Registration is online on the KEA portal, usually announced in January/February for admission in the following academic year.
17. What is the SATS DB ID and where do I get it?
The SATS DB ID is the unique identifier for Karnataka students, available from the student's school. It's needed for synchronization with Education Department databases.
18. If I studied in CBSE/ICSE but in Karnataka, am I eligible for KCET?
Yes, as long as you meet the 7-year study/institution requirement (1st–12th grades) with either 10th or 12th in Karnataka, board doesn't matter.
19. What if my Aadhaar details do not match my certificates?
Update your Aadhaar or school/board records to match before registration and document verification, or you may face rejection at the time of counseling.
20. Do private colleges participate in KCET?
Yes, most major private colleges reserve 40–45% seats for KCET (government quota) and the rest via COMEDK or management quota.
21. What is COMEDK? Can I apply through both KCET and COMEDK?
COMEDK is Consortium of Medical, Dental and Engineering Colleges of Karnataka for non-Karnataka/other students. Karnataka students can write both and use the best rank/option.
22. What if I get a higher fee seat (management/JEE/COMEDK) but then get government quota seat later – will my fees be refunded?
Many colleges (like PES, RV) adjust fees if you move from management/JEE/COMEDK to government quota (KCET), but each college may have its own policy/timelines for refunds or adjustments. Always clarify before paying large sums.
23. What are some reasons KCET registrations/applications might get rejected?
  • Mismatched names/dates across documents
  • Insufficient study certificates/years
  • Improper SATS ID, or mismatching Aadhaar
  • Lack of proper reservation category proof
  • Duplicate registration attempts
24. Where can I find previous year KCET question papers and cutoffs?
KEA website, major bookstores (purchase books), or online platforms like Shiksha, Amazon, and Unacademy provide previous years' KCET papers and mock tests.
25. Does KCET have a separate exam for Computer Science, IT, or Artificial Intelligence branches?
No. All branches are allocated based on the KCET PCM rank. Choice of specialization comes during counseling based on rank and seat availability.
26. If my child studied abroad or in another state mid-way, are they eligible?
If the student cannot show 7 years of study in Karnataka with one board year, they cannot apply through state quota but can use COMEDK (all-India quota).
27. How do I apply for caste/category certificate for KCET?
Apply through local government offices/online Seva portals for your respective category/reservation certificate (e.g., 2A/2B/3A/3B/SC/ST/OBC) before application window closes.
28. What is the meaning of GM, 1G, 2AG, 1R, etc. in cutoff tables?
GM = General Merit, 1G = Category 1 General, 2AG = Category 2A General, 1R = Category 1 Rural, etc. These indicate reservation and subcategories in cutoff/rank tables.
29. How many attempts can I make at KCET?
KCET is generally open to students in the year of their 12th/PUE completion. Multiple attempts are typically not permitted.
30. Can I apply for both KCET and Management quota in the same college?
Yes, you can apply for both KCET (state quota) and management quota. If allotted both, you may need to choose and pay accordingly.
31. How does the choice-filling work in KEA counseling?
After ranks are published, you fill college/branch preferences online, which then get allotted by merit, reservation, and seat availability.
32. How do I correct mistakes after submitting the KCET form?
KEA typically opens a correction window for limited time after application submission where you can edit/correct some details. Always monitor the official portal for dates and instructions.
33. Do all colleges in Bangalore accept KCET ranks?
Most reputed engineering colleges in Bangalore do admit based on KCET, but some private universities (e.g., RVU, PESU, Manipal, Jain University) may have additional quotas/admission routes.
