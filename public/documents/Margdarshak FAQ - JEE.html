JEE Mains (Joint Entrance Examination - Main) FAQ

1. Who is eligible to write JEE Mains?
- Students who have passed/are appearing in 10+2 (or equivalent) with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
- Age is generally not a criterion, but check latest NTA notifications.
2. What is the exam pattern of JEE Mains?
- Computer-based test in Physics, Chemistry, Maths.
- Each paper has 90 questions (30 per subject), with sections A (MCQs) & B (numerical value).
- 4 marks for correct, -1 for wrong answer in MCQs; no negative for numerical.
3. How many times is JEE Mains conducted?
- Typically twice a year (January, April), best score considered for ranking.
4. Which colleges admit through JEE Main?
- NITs, IIITs, GFTIs, many state & private universities.
5. What are typical documents required?
- Aadhaar (with updated biometrics/photo)
- 10th, 12th mark sheets/certificates
- Caste/Category certificates (if applicable)
- Passport-sized photographs and signatures (scanned)
6. What is the difference between qualifying marks & admission cutoff?
- Qualifying: Minimum score required to be eligible for JEE Advanced.
7. Are there reservations/quotas?
- Yes: SC/ST/OBC/EWS/People With Disability as per Government norms.
- Home State/Other State quota in NITs based on class 12 board location

JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) Counselling

1. What is JoSAA?
- The centralized seat allocation system for IITs, NITs, IIITs, and several GFTIs.
2. What documents are required for JoSAA?
- JEE Main/Advanced admit and score cards
- 10th & 12th certificates/mark sheets
- Category/PwD certificates
3. How do I fill choices in JoSAA?
- List of available programs/branches in all participating institutes, sorted by preference.
4. What is the process flow of JoSAA?
  1. Online registration and choice filling
  2. Mock seat allocation rounds for guidance
  3. Locking of choices
  4. Multiple rounds of seat allocation
  5. Seat acceptance, reporting, and document verification at reporting centers
  6. Subsequent rounds: freeze/float/slide options
5. How is the home state quota applied?
- NITs: 50% of seats for candidates who passed 12th from school/institute in that state.

- Domicile or place of birth does not matter; it‚Äôs where you gave the 12th board exam that counts for NIT home quota.
