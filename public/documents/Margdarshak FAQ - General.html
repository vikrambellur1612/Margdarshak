1. Engineering Streams & Courses Discussed

  • Traditional: Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, Electronics & Communication (ECE), Computer Science (CS), Information Technology (IT)
  • Interdisciplinary/Emerging: Mechatronics, Aerospace & Aeronautics, Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), Data Science, Financial Engineering/Fintech, Biotechnology, Biomedical, Food Technology, Computer Science & Design
  • Paths: BE vs B.Tech, BS (Science), Integrated M.Tech in select IIITs

2. Top & Popular Engineering Colleges Mentioned

  • Premier Institutes: IITs, NITs (Suratkal, Trichy, Warangal, etc.), IIITs (Hyderabad, Bangalore, Delhi)
  • Karnataka/Private Focus: PES University (PESU), RV College of Engineering (RVCE), BMSCE, MSRIT, Dayanand Sagar, etc.
  • Universities with Own Entrance: VIT (Vellore, Chennai), Manipal Institute of Technology, Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham, BITS (Pilani, Goa, Hyderabad), SASTRA, Plaksha, Ashoka, others
  • Distinction between older main campuses (e.g., RVCE vs RVU/RVITM; PES RR vs EC campus)

3. Entrance Exams Overview

  • KCET (Karnataka CET): State quota (min 7 yrs study in Karnataka incl. 10th/12th), 50% KCET + 50% PCM board marks, no negative marking, counseling via KEA
  • JEE Main & Advanced: National level for IITs, NITs, IIITs (75% boards or top 20 percentile for eligibility)
  • COMEDK: Private colleges in Karnataka (open to all India)
  • PESSAT: Discontinued; PES now via KCET (~1.25L/year) or JEE/COMEDK (~4-5L/year, direct counseling)
  • University-specific Exams: VITEEE, MET (Manipal), AEEE (Amrita), BITSAT, UGEE (IIIT-H), etc.

4. Admissions & Fees Details

  • PES: Two routes â€“ KCET (lower fees), JEE/COMEDK (higher fees), separate registration for JEE/COMEDK route
  • RVCE: KCET & COMEDK only; no separate entrance or counseling
  • Other colleges (e.g., VIT, Manipal, Amrita): Admission via their own test or JEE, fees and process differ by route & campus

5. Placements & Industry Trends

  • CS & allied branches: higher placement rates and salary but also higher competition
  • Non-CS (Mechanical, Biotech, Aero, etc.): Opportunities are growing, salary curve is gradual, specialized/niche jobs increasing
  • Placements fluctuate with broader market trends; skills and interest drive long-term success
  • New universities like Plaksha reportedly achieved high early placements

6. Additional Tips & Observations

  • BE vs B.Tech: Minimal difference, with B.Tech a bit more practical; "Hons" indicates more credits/courses
  • For some admissions (KCET, BITS, MIT), board marks matter; for JEE admissions, only eligibility (not merit) uses board marks
  • Visit campus, connect with alumni, check curriculum specifics
  • In premier colleges (e.g., IIIT B, IISC), hostel/residential life is often mandatory for all students
  • Fee structure can differ greatly depending on branch, route, and whether via state/national quota
  • Holistic selections gaining traction (including extracurriculars, e.g., Plaksha)
