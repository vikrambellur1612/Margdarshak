Tip #1
Focus on Session 1 of JEE Mains - it's easier to score before board distractions begin.
Tip #2
Start serious preparation from Class 11 - or even Class 9, if possible.
Tip #3
NCERT is critical - especially for Chemistry; revise it thoroughly.
Tip #4
Mock tests are essential - solve many from various sources, starting early.
Tip #5
Avoid guesswork in exams - it usually hurts more than helps.
Tip #6
Prioritize deep understanding of concepts over rote learning.
Tip #7
Don't panic if the paper is tough - it's tough for everyone.
Tip #8
Regularly solve question papers - especially in Physics and Maths.
Tip #9
Switch to JEE Advanced prep early if eligible after Session 1.
Tip #10
Mathematics needs extra effort - beyond NCERT.
Tip #11
Choose school with integrated coaching - helps stay in the right environment.
Tip #12
Avoid distractions like mobile and TV - discipline matters.
Tip #13
Maintain physical and mental health - sleep, exercise, and relaxation are key.
Tip #14
Consistency matters - prepare every day, not just during intense periods.
Tip #15
Get clarity on concepts - don't just memorize formulas.
Tip #16
Time management is crucial - both in preparation and during exams.
Tip #17
Take breaks and avoid burnout - mental health is as important as preparation.
Tip #18
Join good coaching if possible - peer environment and guidance helps.
Tip #19
Regular revision is key - especially in the final months.
Tip #20
Believe in yourself and stay positive - confidence matters.
